My identity, strengths and values

Describe how your culture has influenced your values and identity.

I am from Kerala in India, and my identity has been shaped by my upbringing. Coming from a collectivist culture, who I am today is a culmination of all the values I have learnt and the support I have received from my whānau, teachers and friends. Strong family values, respect for elders and a sense of duty towards my whānau, are some of the values my culture has imbibed in me.

Describe your core personal values.

The values I uphold are living with integrity, being in harmony with other people and the environment, and giving back to the society.

Describe your strengths and limitations.

Strengths Kindness, Respect, Empathy, Resilience, Positive attitude
Limitations Can overload myself and not reach out for help, trying to do things as properly or perfectly as possible, managing conflicts
In the Myers Briggs personality test, I am a Defender ISFJ-T. As per their evaluation, my strengths include being helpful, empathatic, reliable and loyal. The limitations include setting too high standards for oneself, tendency to overload and repressing feelings.

Evaluate your strengths and limitations in terms of your learning and career development. Identify which of your strengths might help you in your learning journey and how they might intersect with learning obstacles.

My strengths make me a kind and empathetic team player. I am a listener and I respect other people's viewpoints. My resilience allows me to see things through to completion inspite of difficulties, which will be particularly advantageous during the bootcamp. With respect to my limitations, as I am aware of them, I shall take conscious measures to ask for help. I also understand the very essence of Agile methodology is to create and refine your product, so I will stick to the timeboxes and move on to the next exercise even if I feel my output isn't as good as I want it to be.
